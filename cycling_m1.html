Genetic Algorithm : first application in civil engineering

During my first research project in 2020 I had to study structure scheduling. The main challenge when building a construction in reinforced concrete is to propose a scheduling that : a) is rapid (time constraint for the builder : the more rapid, the less it costs) b) limits the number of wall forms used for pouring the concrete (financial and logistic aspects) and c) ease the crew movements (practical aspect). As contrary of all those objectives which can be easily handled by a genetic algorithm, there is one particular tricky constraint in construction : the scheduling has to be physically feasible. Therefore, a matrix of constraints is used to inform the algorithm of the feasibility of the scheduling. The figure below describes a scheduling in 4 phases of a very simple floor with 4 rooms.
